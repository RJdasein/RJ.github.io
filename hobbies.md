# Being

Life does not begin outside therapy.

The ways I move, travel, learn, create, and encounter the world continually shape how I accompany others.

Being is where my practice begins.

---

## Movement

Movement has always been my first language.

Over the years, I have explored dance, figure skating, ballet, hiking, strength training, and other embodied practices. Each discipline continues to teach me something about balance, fear, resilience, and presence.

These experiences are not separate from my work—they are part of how I understand the body, emotion, and human connection.

---

## Music

Music offers another way of listening.

I am currently learning the violin and exploring singing, discovering how rhythm, breath, and sound can express what words often cannot.

---

## Places

Living across different countries has become an important part of my personal and professional journey.

China → France → Japan → Indonesia → Malaysia → Vietnam → Germany

Every place has reshaped my understanding of identity, migration, culture, belonging, and psychotherapy.

---

## Photography

I enjoy documenting quiet moments during everyday life and travel—morning light, empty streets, conversations, landscapes, and the subtle beauty that often goes unnoticed.

A selection of photographs will be shared here.
